# Chapter 1

### "Polgar Method"
- Laszlo and Klara wanted to create specialized geniuses
- trained their children in chess from day 1
- children became world-class chess players

### Pattern Recognition
- chess, poker, firefighters are combinations of patterns repeated
- repetition fosters pattern recognition --> learning
- narrow experience = better

- other fields don't have clearly repeating partners
- repetition doesn't foster learning
- broader experience = better

### Domain Specificity
- "kind" learning environments
	- patterns repeat over and over
	- accurate and immediate feedback
	- improve by engaging w/activity and trying to do better
	- experts struggle w/novel situations/environments
- "wicked" learning environments
	- unclear/incomplete rules
	- lack of obvious or repeated patterns
	- delayed/inaccurate feedback
	- experience can reinforce wrong lessons
		- doctor diagnosing cold w/hands --> becomes critically acclaimed for accuracy
		- sticking hand in someone's mouth CAUSED the cold

### Machine and Human
- tactics vs strategy
	- tactics are short term moves
		- immediate/short-term chess combos
	- strategies are long term plans
		- string of combos
		- managing many short-term moves for long term success
	- computers are better at memorizing/seeing combos (short term)
	- humans are better at seeing/planning strategies (long-term)
- centaurs
	- human and bot combo players
	- beat top GMs and chess bots
	- humans direct/manage computer to examine specific moves + synthesize moves
	- humans do what humans do best: synthesize and plan
	- computers do what computers do best: memorize/cover all short-term possible moves or tactics

### Savants/AI
- savants are hyperspecialized in a certain domain
- need to start from young age --> learn and memorize patterns
- typically closed or kind environments
- AI is like a savant because it requires stable structures and narrow worlds (kind env.)

### Top Thinkers
- Nobel laurates are more likely to have avocations outside their field
	- typically creative and performance (poetry, writing, art, theatre, etc.)
- breath supports insights that can't be found from domain-specific expertise alone
